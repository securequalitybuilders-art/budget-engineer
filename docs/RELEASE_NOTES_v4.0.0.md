## Overview

**Budget Engineer v4.0.0** is the first release where the platform becomes a coherent **local-first architecture, engineering, preconstruction, procurement, delivery, and handover system**.

It now combines:

- architecture-aware plan generation
- canonical geometry and validated room/wall/opening logic
- multi-storey and typology-aware planning
- technical drawing and issue-sheet packaging
- trade-detailed BOQ, schedules, programme, and cashflow
- site analysis and heliodon foundations
- interior documentation foundations
- structural and MEP pre-design support
- assurance, delivery, procurement, handover, and project-controls workflows
- export/import continuity and release-hardened engineering gates

This is the release where Budget Engineer moves from being a strong design-costing platform into a broader **professional construction lifecycle platform**.

## What's New in v4.0.0

## 1. Architectural Generator Maturity

The architectural generator is now substantially more capable and stable than earlier versions.

### Major improvements include
- constrained zoning
- adjacency-driven wall logic
- smart openings
- vertical chassis and support-axis logic
- per-storey `floorRole`-aware planning
- apartment unit modelling
- mixed-use circulation and entrance separation
- template-seeded grid packing
- canonical geometry normalization
- overlap rejection and opening-host validation

### Result
The platform now supports much stronger early-stage generation across:
- detached houses
- villas
- duplexes
- apartment blocks
- mixed-use buildings
- clinics
- schools
- warehouse + office
- worship/community halls

- automatic certified code approval

- a fully cloud-native collaboration platform
- a full CAFM / maintenance platform

- professional-grade
- local-first

## Known Limits

The platform is now much more mature, but some limits remain:

- human professional review and signoff are still required
- structural and MEP outputs remain pre-design / review-oriented, not final engineered issue sets
- code/review support is assistive, not authority-certified approval
- advanced cloud collaboration, payment rails, and CAFM/operations remain future layers
- continued calibration against real-world projects will strengthen confidence further
